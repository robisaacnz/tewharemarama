TE WHARE MARAMA

INTERNET POLICY

Te Whare Marama provides computers and Internet access to residents and staff. This service is provided for free, but has some rules that must be followed.

Be nice

Don't use the Internet to threaten, abuse or harass anyone.

Don't use the Internet to access offensive, dangerous, inappropriate or illegal material. This includes downloading pirated music or movies.

Be careful

Don't use the Internet to tell anyone that you are in a refuge or the location of the refuge. Be cautious when disclosing any personal details.

Do report any email, messages or downloaded material which you feel is obscene or makes you feel uncomfortable to a staff member.

Respect others

Don't use the Internet to share any information about staff or other residents, even in private conversations.

Don't upload photos that show the refuge, where the refuge is, or anyone staying at the refuge.

Be responsible for children

As a parent or caregiver, you are responsible for your children's use of the Internet. You must ensure that your children understand these rules.

Children under 14 are prohibited from using Facebook or violating any other social media terms of service.

Agreement

I understand the rules for Internet use.

I agree that if I break these rules, I may lose the right to access the Internet at the refuge.
